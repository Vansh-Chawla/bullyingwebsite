Personal appearance and how it affects teenagers



Personal appearance is how you look in terms of the shape of your body, your posture and gestures. Personal appearance has a big and major impact on teenagers and their self-esteem. Self esteem is the overall opinion about how a person feels about themselves. Having a low self esteem means the person doesn't like how they are, especially in terms of their personal appearance.

In this generation, teenagers have a habit of comparing how they look with people around them, especially in school. This can make them feel bad about themselves which is directly related to their self-esteem. Low self-esteem and poor body image are the causes for the development of risky weight loss strategies, eating disorders and mental health disorders such as depression. Teenagers tend to change their eating habits to look better. Sudden change of eating habits can lead to feeling tired, getting ill, hair loss and skin problems. Social media has a big effect on how teenagers feel about their looks and body. Social media platforms such as snapchat have filters which can change the way people look. This can be addictive and make teenagers feel bad about how they really look in real life.

If your child has low self esteem because of their personal appearance, you can help them by giving them some motivation, by loving them unconditionally, by not scolding them for failure and by helping them gain new skills. You can also talk to them about how they feel everyday and comfort them. This can help them gain confidence about themselves.

Peer Pressure

What is peer pressure?

Peer pressure is when teenagers are influenced by other people especially in school to act in a certain way. Peer pressure is also when teenagers do something or act in a different way to fit in with different people. These days teenagers are influenced a lot by other people so they get accepted and valued by their friends. They also do it to increase their social status in school. The biggest cause of peer pressure is low self esteem as teenagers having a low self esteem have a tendency to be with people having a higher self esteem than them. Peer pressure is most commonly found in the ages of 12-19 years old.

How peer pressure impacts teenagers?

Peer pressure can lead teenagers in bad directions which includes trying alcohol or drugs, skipping school etc. This has a big impact on the teenager’s future in terms of their health and safety. Peer pressure decreases self confidence and can lead to poor academic performance. Teenagers going through peer pressure have a tendency of distancing themselves from their family members which can lead to depression and anxiety. This can lead teenagers to engage in self harm and suicidal thoughts.




How can we solve this problem?

If your child who is a teenager is going through peer pressure, there are a lot of things you can do to help them. You can help them by letting them know that you are there for them and are there to listen and help if they need it. You can also share your own experiences of peer pressure which can make them feel that they aren't the only one struggling and getting influenced easily. Finally you can also teach them how to deal with people who aren't good for them which can improve their social life and decrease the chance of getting influenced by other people.

Real life examples of peer pressure:

Convincing a friend to skip school
Convincing someone to buy e-cigarettes
Pressuring a friend to drink or try drugs
Encouraging a peer to fight or bully someone

























Social Media



This generation is nothing without social media platforms. Social media is used to communicate and share information amongst different people all around the world. Social media platforms are a great way to increase your social skills as it allows you to communicate with people of any country. In this era, social media is very common amongst teenagers which has its both negatives and positives. Teenagers use social media to communicate with each other about school work, homeworks, projects etc. Through social media, teenagers also stay aware about the things happening in the world. Additionally social media can create peer motivation, inspire the youth to develop healthy habits, and motivate teenagers to try something new. Social media can also help teens find positive role models online.


However social media also has some negative effects on teenagers. Social media can distract children from their academics, disturb their sleep patterns, expose them to bullying, and can give them unrealistic views of other people’s lives. Social media can also lead to rumors about a person which has a big impact on their mental health as it can lead to depression. Along with that, social media has a big contribution on a teenager’s self esteem. Social media can trigger comparison with others which can make teenagers feel bad about themselves and how they look. This will then impact their self esteem which also has a huge impact on their mental health. This can lead to depression, anxiety and mood swings. This can also impact the eating habits of a teenager which has a huge effect on the person’s health. As parents, you should allow your children who are teenagers to use social media however teach them how to use it in the correct way. You should also put a time limit on their social media usage so they don't get addicted.
